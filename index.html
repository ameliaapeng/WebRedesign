History and Culture of Beijing

Beijing has been a major cultural and political center for over 3,000 years. It became the capital during the Yuan Dynasty (1271–1368), and remained the capital under the Ming and Qing Dynasties. Known for landmarks like the Forbidden City, Tiananmen Square, and parts of the Great Wall, Beijing has long been the heart of Chinese politics, tradition, and innovation. Today, it’s a blend of ancient history and modern vibrance—an iconic city that represents both the past and future of China.
